Write about a default that you've been dealing with all your life that you have had to unlearn.

I have always found myself being directed towards "being nice". Directed by teachers, parental figures, media, and practically every agent of civilisation for a young person. 

Being nice is good, it maintains harmony, it ensures we don't break surface tension. My problem is that it reveals nothing about what goes on underneath. It feels like it is a veil that covers all sorts of anti-social or violent* tendencies that bubble up inside.

\* *The term violence here is being used to broadly describe violence of all shapes. Physical, emotional, situational, social, etc. Any kind of activity that reverberates destructively throughout a system and needs to be addressed in real time is being termed violence.*

It's like an Instagram filter we slap across society that removes our ability to judge anyone's true colors. 

I have a cousin, one might describe him as an "asshole". He speaks his mind. His opinion about the world and about you is as transparent as things get. My sister hated him when they first met. She thought he was rude or crass or loud. But over time she realised he has a good heart, he was just missing the "Be nice" filter. He was extremely helpful, and usually just said what everyone was thinking. If you don't understand his operating system it's easy to confuse his language for his intent, because most people use language as a weapon. It always serves as a means to an end. But the words he uses are an end in themselves. You have no need to be defensive because he isn't building up to anything. 

I get the whole "It's a dangerous thing to mistake speaking without thought for speaking the truth" - Benoit Blanc. 

But imagine these mental categories. There are people with ulterior motives and those without. Both are forced to present "nice-ness" in order to ensure their listeners don't feel alarmed or in danger in case their words are building up to a one-two punch. But what if we could early in our conversations just identify who has ulterior motives for their words and who doesn't, and then the ones without can just shed their filter. 

I think being nice and processing niceness takes a lot of mental load, we run a complicated post-processing algorithm to encode niceness in our speech and our listener runs a pre-process to decode niceness. If we did away with it our world would present itself more rough around the edges but it does away with so much processing.

If we build a society that maximises on niceness instead of kindness or empathy or understanding we are only building the veneer of a socially sound society, the reward function needs to be readdressed. Niceness gets us to survive next to each other, honesty gets us to understand each other.
